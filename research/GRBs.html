A bit of a Primer

Below is some of my preliminary work for fitting data with boxfit.  Most of these are still preliminary and are subject to change.

Each GRB is listed by name and the datasets attached should also be identified.  All of the fits are attempting to identify parameters that explain the lightcurves at all observed wavelengths using boxfit.  Parameters should be identified in the hover-over feature on the embedded plots.  All plots were produced using the bokeh python package which converts python plots into javascript enabled html pages.

Synchotron-Self Compton radiation features.

GRB modeling has been temporarily been put on hold as we are currently working on adding another radiation feature to the boxfit routine.  Currently, boxfit focuses purely on synchotron emission because it is assumed that the cooling timescale of the burst is much shorter than the dynamic timescale (ie: thermal emission does not occur because of the density of the medium, and we do not have to deal with convection because the dynamics of the burst are too fast for energy to be lost through this method compared to the efficiency of synchotron radiation).

Synchotron-Self Compton, is still a possibility, though because there only needs to be a sufficient number of accelerated electrons to scatter the emitted photons at certain energies (There is a range of energies above what is called the cooling frequency where this can occur, but there is an upper bound as an electron can only impart so much energy to a photon, so electrons above a certain energy are not effected.)  I am hoping to do a more general write-up of this mechanism, but for now, I will link to the paper I am using to implement the feature.  I apologize if the paper is paywalled, and it is available open elsewhere, but I wanted to link to an official location for it.

Currently, I have the lower bound on slow cooling implemented, and it seems to be working well,  the next step is to impose the upper edge, as well as begin looking at the computational complexity of establishing the other regimes.  Technically, IC depends on a parameter, Y, which is calculated numerically from fluid conditions, but we are really trying to walk before we run here, and want to avoid overloading a program with too many serial operations before we have figured out a proper way to optimize.

GRB 070125

The burst that was the impetus for all of this.  GRB 070125 is a gamma-ray burst that went off in 2007--about 10 years ago at the time of writing.   The reason why this burst is still being talked about is because of how hard it has been to lock down the parameters.  The radio lightcurves in particular are the main culprits.  Unlike in the higher frequencies, they are all quite flat for a long period of time, not breaking until nearly 100 days. 

Now, we can model this behavior in boxfit, but then we run into the issue of the there not being enough signal in the x-ray regime, or too much in x-ray and way too much in the optical.  Our most recent fits seem to have us quite close, but we have held off until finishing the implemenation of SSC so that we feel confident that we have a complete model of the blast wave emission.
